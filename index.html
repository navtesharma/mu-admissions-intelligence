Critical findings from this cycle's data
Critical
53.9% drop at Stage 1 entry
Applicants who start the form but don't complete Stage 1 cite MUSAT prep uncertainty as the #1 reason in exit surveys. A free 2-week MUSAT prep resource sent at form-start could recover ~180 additional qualified applicants this cycle.
+₹4.2Cr revenue potential
Opportunity
Alumni referrals convert 5.4× better than Instagram
Alumni-referred applicants show 4.9% CVR vs 0.7% for Instagram at 1/10th the CAC. Current alumni referral program has no structured incentive. A structured ambassador program targeting 200 alumni could generate 400+ high-quality leads.
Lowest CAC channel, most underinvested
Watch
Fee payment friction causing 24.8% offer drop-off
Of 310 offers extended, 77 did not enrol. Exit interviews show 61% cited payment deadline inflexibility. An EMI-at-offer-stage option or 2-week extension for scholarship recipients could recover 40–50 seats.
+₹9.3Cr if 40 seats recovered
Opportunity
Engineer + Startup profiles over-index on placement CTC
This cluster (38% of batch) produces 58% of placements above ₹40L. Current intake process doesn't actively filter for startup experience at Stage 1. Adding a 1-question startup involvement filter could improve average cohort CTC by 8–12%.
Strengthens placement brand
Why I Built This
Problem statement · Rationale · Expansion vision
MU Admissions Intelligence
Built for Masters' Union's Founder's Office. Not a generic dashboard — a tool designed around the specific operational decisions MU's leadership makes every admissions cycle.
The Problem I'm Solving
Masters' Union is one of India's most ambitious business schools. But ambition doesn't fix operational blind spots. The admissions team makes high-stakes decisions every cycle — which channels to invest in, how many seats to reserve for scholarships, when to intervene in the funnel. Right now, those decisions are made from instinct and spreadsheets. This dashboard changes that. It makes the admissions funnel visible, quantifies every drop-off point, and tells the Founder's Office exactly where to act — and what it's worth if they do. The 53.9% drop at Stage 1 alone represents hundreds of qualified applicants who didn't make it past the first hurdle. The ₹4.2Cr recovery potential isn't hypothetical — it's the math of knowing where the leak is.
Why AI, Not Just Analytics
Traditional dashboards show you what happened. This dashboard tells you what to do next. The AI Analyst layer isn't cosmetic. It's trained on MU's specific data — funnel stages, source CVRs, scholarship correlations, profile clusters — and answers the questions a Founder's Office actually asks. Not "how many applied" but "where should we spend our next ₹10L to get the best students." This is the difference between reporting and intelligence. MU doesn't need more data. It needs better decisions from the data it already has.
Long-Term Expansion
This is Version 1. The infrastructure supports a much larger vision: → Placement Intelligence: Track which profiles produce the highest CTC. Feed that signal back into admissions criteria in real time. → Corporate Partnership Tracker: Monitor which live projects with Microsoft, Flipkart, PwC are active, expiring, or underperforming. → City Expansion Signals: Geographic data already shows where MU has demand without supply — Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad. → Alumni Engagement Score: Alumni are the highest-converting referral channel. Tracking engagement creates a predictive model for next cycle's lead quality. → Fee Sensitivity Modelling: Test scholarship allocation scenarios and see projected revenue impact before committing.
Why I Built This Specifically
I built Evolve DSC from 4 to 100+ members at Delhi University. In that process I ran admissions of a kind — convincing students to join, tracking drop-off in interest, figuring out which channels converted and which didn't. The problem is identical to MU's, just at a different scale. MU's Founder's Office makes decisions that determine the quality of every cohort, the revenue of every cycle, and the reputation of the school for the next decade. Those decisions deserve better infrastructure than a Google Sheet and gut feel. This is what I would have built on Day 1 if I'd joined MU's Founder's Office.
